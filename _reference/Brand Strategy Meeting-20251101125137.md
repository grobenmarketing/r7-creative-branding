# Brand Strategy Meeting

**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Meeting Type:** Implementation Phase 1 - Brand Foundation
* * *
## Meeting Agenda
* * *
### 1\. Core Brand Questions (35 min)
### **THE 10 INTERVIEW QUESTIONS FOR BRAND PILLAR BUILDING**
#### **PART 1: FOCUS & POSITIONING (Questions 1-3)**
_These feed: Brand Positioning Statement + Target Market Definition_
**1\. "What type of work do you WANT more of, and what do you wish you could stop doing?"**
_Why this works:_ People immediately tell you their ideal vs. current reality. Gets you positioning without asking "who's your ideal client?"
_Listen for:_
*   Specific services/products they want to focus on
*   Dollar amounts and project types
*   What they're trying to move away from
*   Language about "those clients" vs. "these clients"
_Feeds these pillars:_ Brand Positioning, Target Market Definition
* * *
**2\. "Describe your absolute favorite client or project from the last year. What made it great?"**
_Why this works:_ Gets you a real example of their ideal, not a theoretical answer. Shows what they value.
_Listen for:_
*   Type of client/industry
*   Project characteristics
*   What made it smooth/profitable/enjoyable
*   What that client understood or valued
_Feeds these pillars:_ Target Market Definition, Customer Avatar
* * *
**3\. "When that type of client comes to you, what situation are they usually in? What's going on in their business that made them realize they need you?"**
_Why this works:_ Gets you the trigger moment and context. Shows you the customer's world.
_Listen for:_
*   Specific business situations
*   Pain points and frustrations
*   Timeline/urgency factors
*   What they've already tried
_Feeds these pillars:_ Target Market Definition, Problem Solved Statement, Customer Avatar
* * *
#### **PART 2: PROBLEMS & TRANSFORMATION (Questions 4-6)**
_These feed: Problem Solved Statement + Unique Value Proposition + Customer Avatar_
**4\. "Walk me through a recent project from start to finish. What did the client come in wanting, what did you actually deliver, and what changed for them?"**
_Why this works:_ Forces a specific story with beginning, middle, end. Gets transformation naturally.
_Listen for:_
*   Initial problem/need
*   Your process and approach
*   Specific deliverables
*   Impact on their business
*   What they said after
_Feeds these pillars:_ Problem Solved Statement, UVP, Customer Avatar
* * *
**5\. "What's the cost to your ideal clients if they DON'T do this, or if they do it badly? Not just money - what else suffers?"**
_Why this works:_ Gets emotional and practical stakes. Shows what's really at risk.
_Listen for:_
*   Lost opportunities
*   Wasted time/money
*   Stress and frustration
*   Impact on their reputation
*   Competitive disadvantage
_Feeds these pillars:_ Problem Solved Statement, Customer Avatar
* * *
**6\. "What do clients NOT understand about what you do until after they work with you? What's the 'aha moment' they have?"**
_Why this works:_ Reveals hidden value and education gaps. Shows your unique insight.
_Listen for:_
*   Misconceptions about the industry
*   Underestimated value
*   Process they didn't know existed
*   Difference between you and competitors
*   What surprised them
_Feeds these pillars:_ UVP, Problem Solved Statement, Messaging Framework
* * *
#### **PART 3: DIFFERENTIATION (Questions 7-8)**
_These feed: Brand Positioning + UVP + Messaging Framework_
**7\. "Why do your best clients choose you over other options? What did they tell you, or what did you notice they cared about?"**
_Why this works:_ Gets differentiation from CUSTOMER perspective, not yours. Uses their actual words.
_Listen for:_
*   Specific reasons clients mentioned
*   What they valued enough to pay for
*   Comparisons they made
*   Decision factors
*   Actual client quotes if possible
_Feeds these pillars:_ Brand Positioning, UVP, Messaging Framework
* * *
**8\. "What's something you do as part of your normal process that your competitors either skip or charge extra for?"**
_Why this works:_ Gets specific operational differentiation. Shows value delivery others don't match.
_Listen for:_
*   Process differences
*   Extra services included
*   Quality standards
*   Time invested
*   Things they won't compromise on
_Feeds these pillars:_ Brand Positioning, UVP
* * *
#### **PART 4: OBJECTIONS & MESSAGING (Questions 9-10)**
_These feed: Customer Avatar + Messaging Framework_
**9\. "What's the biggest hesitation or objection you hear before someone decides to work with you, and what do they not realize yet?"**
_Why this works:_ Identifies the perception gap. Shows what messaging needs to overcome.
_Listen for:_
*   Price objections and what's behind them
*   Misconceptions about the service
*   Fears or concerns
*   What they're comparing you to
*   Education needed
_Feeds these pillars:_ Customer Avatar, Messaging Framework
* * *
**10\. "If you could get inside your ideal client's head and make them deeply understand ONE thing about working with you - what they'll actually get or how it will help them - what would it be?"**
_Why this works:_ Forces them to prioritize their core message. Customer-benefit focused.
_Listen for:_
*   The transformation, not features
*   Emotional + practical benefits
*   What matters most to them
*   Their passionate language
*   What they wish clients knew upfront
_Feeds these pillars:_ Messaging Framework, UVP
* * *
### 2\. Content Capabilities Assessment (5 min)
**Questions:**
*   "Who on your team is comfortable capturing video/photos on their phone?"
*   "What does a typical work day look like? When are you busiest?"
*   "Do you have good lighting at your location, or mostly indoor/outdoor work?"
*   "Are there times when capturing content would be disruptive to customers?"
_What this tells you: Who to assign as Content Owners, best capture times, location constraints_
* * *
### 3\. Social Media Current State (3 min)
**Questions:**
*   "Which social platforms are you currently on?" (even if inactive)
*   "Do you have access to all your accounts, or are some locked/forgotten?"
*   "What's your Google Business star rating?"
*   "Have you posted anything in the last 6 months? What kind of content?"
_What this tells you: Scope of profile optimization work, which credentials you'll need_
* * *
### 4\. Content Boundaries & Comfort (3 min)
**Questions:**
*   "Is there anything you DON'T want us to show?" (proprietary processes, certain employees, etc.)
*   "Are employees comfortable being on camera, or do you prefer behind-the-scenes/process shots?"
*   "Do you have any brand guidelines or things we should avoid?"
*   "Any competitors you really DON'T want to be like in your content?"
_What this tells you: Content boundaries, employee participation willingness, brand guardrails_
* * *
### 5\. Success Expectations (3 min)
**Questions:**
*   "What would make this a home run for you in 90 days?"
*   "How do you currently get most of your leads?" (referrals, Google, walk-ins, etc.)
*   "What do you hope changes once your content is active?"
_What this tells you: Their definition of success, where content fits in lead funnel, realistic expectations_
* * *
### 6\. Wrap-Up & Next Steps (6 min)
**Cover:**
*   Recap what you learned (key themes)
*   Schedule Content Strategy meeting: "Let's get your Content Strategy meeting on the calendar for about a week from now. I'll send you a scheduling link."
*   Set expectations: "You'll receive your Brand Pillars document within 2 business days. Review it before our Content Strategy meeting, where we'll use it to plan your first 3 weeks of content."
*   Answer questions
* * *
